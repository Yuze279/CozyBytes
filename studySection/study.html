Note Taking Methods

“Cornell Method”

A structure that allowed for easy review and self-assessment.

Organizes notes into a structured layout with separate sections for main points, keywords, and summaries, promoting active engagement and effective review.



"Mind Mapping"

Utilizes visual representations with a central idea and branching subtopics
to create a dynamic and interconnected note-taking approach,
fostering creativity and idea organization.



"Outline Method"

Presents information hierarchically using headings and bullet points, providing a clear and organized format for capturing key concepts and their relationships.
